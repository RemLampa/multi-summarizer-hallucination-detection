Title: Eyewitness Description of the Iceberg

Witnesses later described the iceberg as a massive formation extending far along the side of the vessel. The collision itself was described as a glancing blow rather than a violent crash, which contributed to the lack of immediate alarm among passengers.

Crew members on watch reported the sighting moments before impact, but the speed of the ship and limited time made avoidance impossible. The damage, though not outwardly dramatic, proved catastrophic as it compromised the watertight integrity of the ship.

Source: U.S. Senate Inquiry Testimonies (Public Domain)
