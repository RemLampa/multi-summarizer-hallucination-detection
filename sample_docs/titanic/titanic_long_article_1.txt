Title: The Collision and Realization of Danger

On the night of April 14, 1912, the Titanic struck an iceberg in the North Atlantic. At first, the impact seemed slight, and many passengers continued their activities unaware of the seriousness of the situation. However, officers soon determined that the ship had sustained fatal damage, with water entering multiple compartments.

As the crew assessed the damage, orders were quietly issued to prepare the lifeboats. Passengers were summoned to the decks, many still unconvinced that the great ship could sink. The calm demeanor of the crew initially prevented widespread panic, but the gravity of the situation became increasingly clear as time passed.

Source: Compiled from British Wreck Commissioner’s Inquiry (Public Domain)
