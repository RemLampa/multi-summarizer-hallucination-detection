Title: Conditions in the Lifeboats

Those who escaped in lifeboats faced hours in the cold Atlantic night. With limited supplies and overcrowded conditions, survival depended on endurance and cooperation.

Passengers recalled the eerie silence after the ship disappeared, broken only by distant cries. Many lifeboats hesitated to return for fear of being overwhelmed, a decision that would later be widely debated.

Source: Inquiry Testimonies (Public Domain)
