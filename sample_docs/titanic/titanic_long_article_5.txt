Title: Aftermath and Calls for Reform

The sinking of the Titanic shocked the world and led to widespread calls for reform in maritime safety. Investigations revealed shortcomings in lifeboat provisions, communication systems, and emergency preparedness.

As a result, new regulations were introduced to ensure that ships carried sufficient lifeboats for all passengers and maintained continuous wireless communication. The tragedy served as a turning point in maritime history, influencing safety standards for generations to come.
