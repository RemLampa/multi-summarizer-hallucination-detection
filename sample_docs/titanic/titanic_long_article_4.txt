Title: The Launching of Lifeboats

As the situation became more urgent, lifeboats were lowered into the sea. At first, the process was orderly, with women and children given priority. However, confusion grew as passengers realized that there were not enough boats for everyone.

Some lifeboats were launched partially filled, a decision that later drew criticism. Officers worked to maintain discipline, but the increasing tilt of the ship and the realization of impending disaster created a sense of urgency among those still on board.

Source: Archibald Gracie, The Truth about the Titanic (Public Domain)
