Title: A Passenger’s Experience on Board

Passengers in the first-class sections noted the absence of any dramatic jolt. Many continued reading or conversing, unaware that the ship had been mortally wounded. It was only after stewards began advising passengers to put on lifebelts that concern began to spread.

Even then, some hesitated to leave the comfort of the ship for the uncertainty of lifeboats. The belief in the Titanic’s safety and reputation contributed to delays in evacuation, which later proved fatal for many.

Source: Lawrence Beesley, The Loss of the S.S. Titanic (Public Domain)
