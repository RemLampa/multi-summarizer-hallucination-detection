Title: Wireless Messages and Calls for Help

As the severity of the Titanic's condition became clear, wireless operators began sending distress signals into the night. The call for assistance reached several nearby vessels, though distance and ice conditions delayed any immediate rescue.

The persistence of the operators ensured that help would eventually arrive, even as the ship continued to take on water. Their efforts were later recognized as crucial in saving many lives, as the Carpathia responded to the distress calls and made its way through the icy waters.

Source: Public Domain Maritime Inquiry Reports
